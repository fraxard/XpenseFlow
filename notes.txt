for turning this into something mini-SaaS worthy — here's how I'd think about it in tiers:
Foundational (do these regardless of direction)

        Categories/tags per transaction (Food, Rent, Salary, etc.) with a dropdown, not free text
        Edit existing transactions, not just delete
        Date field per transaction (right now nothing is timestamped)
        Form validation beyond alert() — inline error states
        Currency formatting/selection instead of hardcoded $

Makes it actually useful

Filter/search history by category, date range, or type
Monthly view with month-over-month comparison
Charts — spending by category (pie), balance over time (line) — this is the single highest-impact addition for "feels like a product"
Recurring transactions (rent, subscriptions) that auto-add
Budgets per category with progress bars / over-budget warnings
CSV/PDF export of transactions

What makes it a SaaS, not just a nicer local app

Real backend + database (currently everything lives in localStorage, which means no cross-device sync and data loss risk)
User accounts/auth so data isn't tied to one browser
Multi-currency and multi-account support (checking, credit card, cash)
Shared/household budgets (multiple users on one set of finances)
Bank sync via Plaid or similar (this is the big one competitors like YNAB/Mint live on)
Email/push notifications ("You're at 90% of your Dining budget")
Tiered pricing (free tier: manual entry only; paid: bank sync, multi-account, exports)
