What makes it a SaaS, not just a nicer local app

Real backend + database (currently everything lives in localStorage, which means no cross-device sync and data loss risk)
User accounts/auth so data isn't tied to one browser
Multi-currency and multi-account support (checking, credit card, cash)
Shared/household budgets (multiple users on one set of finances)
Bank sync via Plaid or similar (this is the big one competitors like YNAB/Mint live on)
Email/push notifications ("You're at 90% of your Dining budget")
Tiered pricing (free tier: manual entry only; paid: bank sync, multi-account, exports)
